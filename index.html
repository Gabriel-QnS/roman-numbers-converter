About Roman Numbers

Roman numerals are symbols used in a numerical notation system based on ancient Rome, representing fixed integer values.

They can't portray zero (0) or any value above 3999 without workarounds.

This limit hindered mathematical advance, and it was eventually replaced by the Hindu-Arabic system.

Despite this it served the people of the Roman empire for about 1800 years.
